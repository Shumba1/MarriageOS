# Purpose

Help a user identify when touch has become predictably loaded with escalation pressure and give them a fast, bond-protecting first intervention.

# Audience

Couples with remaining goodwill where one person experiences contact as loaded and the other experiences non-response as rejection.

# Output requirements

The final repair module must follow the standard 7-part anatomy:
1. What’s Happening
2. What Makes It Worse
3. Do This First
4. Say This
5. Do This Tonight
6. 7-Day Reset
7. Escalate If…

# Required claims

- Pressure can shrink the bond even when the initiating partner feels calm.
- Touch becomes aversive when it is predictably linked to escalation.
- A bond-protecting pause must avoid coldness or punishment.
- “No + repair” is a core stabilising rule.

# Required cautions / exclusions

- Do not frame this as entitlement.
- Do not imply that affection must earn sex.
- Do not moralise libido differences.

# Tone rules

- calm
- practical
- emotionally adult
- non-moralising
- clear under stress

# Canonical constraints

- Repair is the centre of V1.
- The module must be symptom-led, not theory-led.
- The output must sound like a premium operator, not a therapist worksheet.

Why it matters: It preserves dignity on both sides.
Use in final: Say This / 7-Day Reset.
